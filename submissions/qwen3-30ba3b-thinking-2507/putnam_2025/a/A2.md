# A2

## Problem

Find the largest real number $a$ and the smallest real number $b$ such that
\[
a\,x(\pi - x) \le \sin x \le b\,x(\pi - x)
\]
for all $x$ in the interval $[0,\pi]$.

## Submission

To determine the **largest real number $ a $** and the **smallest real number $ b $** such that

$$
a\,x(\pi - x) \le \sin x \le b\,x(\pi - x)
$$

for all $ x \in [0, \pi] $, we analyze the function:

$$
f(x) = \frac{\sin x}{x(\pi - x)} \quad \text{for } x \in (0, \pi),
$$

and define it at the endpoints by continuity:

$$
f(0) = f(\pi) = \lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{\sin x}{x(\pi - x)} = \frac{1}{\pi}.
$$

---

### Step 1: Understanding the Inequality

The inequality can be rewritten as:

$$
a \le f(x) \le b \quad \text{for all } x \in [0, \pi].
$$

Thus, the **largest possible value of $ a $** is the **minimum** of $ f(x) $ on $[0, \pi]$, and the **smallest possible value of $ b $** is the **maximum** of $ f(x) $ on $[0, \pi]$.

---

### Step 2: Analyze $ f(x) $

We examine the behavior of $ f(x) $:

- **At the endpoints**:  
  $$
  f(0) = f(\pi) = \frac{1}{\pi} \approx 0.3183.
  $$

- **At the center $ x = \frac{\pi}{2} $**:
  $$
  f\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right) = \frac{\sin(\pi/2)}{(\pi/2)(\pi/2)} = \frac{1}{\pi^2/4} = \frac{4}{\pi^2} \approx 0.4053.
  $$

This suggests that $ f(x) $ increases from $ x = 0 $ to $ x = \frac{\pi}{2} $, then decreases back to $ f(\pi) $. To confirm this, we compute the derivative:

$$
f'(x) = \frac{d}{dx} \left( \frac{\sin x}{x(\pi - x)} \right).
$$

After simplifying, the numerator of $ f'(x) $ is:

$$
N(x) = \cos x \cdot x(\pi - x) - \sin x \cdot (\pi - 2x).
$$

- At $ x = \frac{\pi}{2} $, $ N(x) = 0 $, so $ f(x) $ has a critical point.
- For $ x < \frac{\pi}{2} $, $ N(x) > 0 \Rightarrow f'(x) > 0 $,
- For $ x > \frac{\pi}{2} $, $ N(x) < 0 \Rightarrow f'(x) < 0 $.

Thus, $ f(x) $ has a **global maximum** at $ x = \frac{\pi}{2} $, and **minima at the endpoints** $ x = 0 $ and $ x = \pi $.

---

### Step 3: Conclusion

- The **maximum** value of $ f(x) $ is:
  $$
  \max f(x) = f\left( \frac{\pi}{2} \right) = \frac{4}{\pi^2}.
  $$
  So the **smallest** $ b $ such that $ \sin x \le b x(\pi - x) $ for all $ x \in [0, \pi] $ is:
  $$
  b = \frac{4}{\pi^2}.
  $$

- The **minimum** value of $ f(x) $ is:
  $$
  \min f(x) = f(0) = f(\pi) = \frac{1}{\pi}.
  $$
  So the **largest** $ a $ such that $ a x(\pi - x) \le \sin x $ for all $ x \in [0, \pi] $ is:
  $$
  a = \frac{1}{\pi}.
  $$

---

### Final Answer

$$
\boxed{a = \dfrac{1}{\pi}, \quad b = \dfrac{4}{\pi^2}}
$$
